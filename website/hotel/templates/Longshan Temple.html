Manka Longshan Temple is a temple in Wanhua District, Taipei, Taiwan.
The temple was built in Taipei in 1738 by settlers from Fujian.
It served as a place of worship and a gathering place for the Chinese settlers.
It is a temple of the Chinese folk religion for worshiping deities such as Guanyin, Mazu and Guan Yu.
