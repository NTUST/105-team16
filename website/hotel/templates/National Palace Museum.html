The National Palace Museum and the Palace Museum in the Forbidden City in Beijing, mainland China, share the same roots.
The old Palace Museum in Beijing split in two as a result of the Chinese Civil War, which divided China into the two entities of the Republic of China on the island of Taiwan and the People's Republic of China on the mainland respectively.
In English, the institution in Taipei is distinguished from the one in Beijing by the additional "National" designation.
In common usage in Chinese, the institution in Taipei is known as the "Taipei Former Palace", while that in Beijing is known as the "Beijing Former Palace".
